exousia (ex-ou-SEEAH) n.

From Koine Greek ἐξοντ- (exont-, participle) +‎ -ῐ́ᾱ (-íā, verbal adjective suffix), from ἔξεστι (éxesti, “it is possible, it is allowed”).
1. power, means, license, or authority to do something.

"All things are subject to interpretation. Whichever interpretation prevails at a given time is a function, not of truth, but of power."
- - Friedrich Nietzsche, Will to Power
